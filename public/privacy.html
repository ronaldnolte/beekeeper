Privacy Policy

BeekTools Beekeeper · Last updated: June 19, 2026

BeekTools Beekeeper ("the App," "we," "us") helps beekeepers track their apiaries, hives, and local nectar-flow conditions. This policy explains what information the App collects, how it is used, and the choices you have. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for advertising.

Information We Collect

  • Account information: the email address you use to create an account and sign in. Authentication is handled by our backend provider (Supabase).
  • Apiary information: the names, locations (latitude/longitude and/or ZIP code), and notes you enter for your apiaries.
  • Beekeeping records: the hives, inspections, interventions, tasks, varroa tests, and related notes you choose to record in the App.
  • Photos: images you choose to take or attach to an inspection. Photos are resized and stored privately in your account; only you can access them.
  • Voice notes: short audio recordings you choose to make on an inspection. Audio is recorded only after you tap the microphone button and grant microphone permission. It is used to produce a text transcript and is then deleted (see “How Voice Notes Are Processed” below).
  • Usage and analytics data: basic information about how the App is used — such as screens viewed, taps, and approximate (non-precise) location derived from your IP address — collected through Google Analytics to help us understand and improve the App. This is not linked to your beekeeping records.

How We Use Your Information

  • To provide and maintain your account and store the records you create.
  • To calculate the Nectar Flow Index and forecasts for your apiary locations.
  • To respond to support and data requests you send us.
  • To understand how the App is used and improve it, through analytics.

How Apiary Locations Are Used

To compute the Nectar Flow Index and weather forecasts, the App sends the coordinates of your apiary (not your identity) to two external data services:

  • Google Earth Engine — to retrieve Sentinel-2 satellite vegetation imagery for the area around your apiary.
  • Open-Meteo — to retrieve historical and forecast weather for that location.

These requests contain only location coordinates and date ranges — no account or personal identifiers are sent to these services.

How Voice Notes Are Processed

When you record a voice note, the audio is sent to Google’s Gemini AI service to be transcribed into text. As soon as transcription succeeds, the audio recording is permanently deleted from our storage — only the resulting text transcript is kept, and you can edit or delete it. If transcription fails, the audio is retained so you can play it back or try again, until you delete it. We do not use your recordings or transcripts to train AI models.

Service Providers

We rely on the following providers to operate the App:

  • Supabase — account authentication and database storage.
  • Vercel — application and API hosting.
  • Google Earth Engine and Open-Meteo — vegetation and weather data, as described above.
  • Google Gemini — transcription of voice notes into text, as described above.
  • Google Analytics — usage and analytics data, as described above, to help us understand and improve the App.

Data Retention

We retain your account and the records you create — including photos — for as long as your account is active. Voice-note audio is deleted automatically once it has been transcribed, as described above. You can delete individual photos, voice notes, and transcripts within the App, and you can request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time (see Account Deletion).

Your Choices and Rights

  • You may view and edit your apiary and hive records within the App at any time.
  • You may request a copy of your data, or its deletion, by contacting us.

Children's Privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect their personal information.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
